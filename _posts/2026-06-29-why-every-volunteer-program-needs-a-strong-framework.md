## Your Volunteer Program Needs a Framework—Here's Why

Running a volunteer program without structure is like building a house without a blueprint.

A volunteer framework outlines the essential elements of your program—from recruitment and onboarding to supervision and recognition. It ensures consistency, saves time, and supports growth.

A good framework includes:

- Program goals and mission alignment
- Clear role descriptions
- Onboarding and training plans
- Communication touchpoints
- Volunteer engagement calendar (socials, training, recognition)
- Retention plan
- Recognition and appreciation plan
- Exit and feedback process

### Why it matters:

When volunteers know what to expect (and what's expected), they perform better and feel more confident.
